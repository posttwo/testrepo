Our Data

By just looking at the data multiple quesitons can be formed, is crime going down, are certain areas more prone to crime than others?

I will try to answer these three questions:

  • What is the most dangerous area in Ireland
  • Historically, how did the crime in this area change?
  • How do the differnet crimes correlate to eachother, does more of one crime mean more of another?

Method

To answer these questions I will seperate the data into the 4 provinces of Ireland: Leinster, Munster, Ulster and Connacht on which I will conduct various statistics.

The location of the Garda Station is a nominal variable, while all the crime figures are intervals.

Closer look at Leinster

Let us take a closer look at Leinster, to see how Leinster changed over the years, and which parts are the most dangerous. Were first going to take a look at the Distribution of Crimes between different Garda Divisions in Leinster

From this graph we can clearly see that Dublin North Central had the most crimes in Leinster in 2016, totalling up at 4783! Looking closer at Dublin North Central we can see that it covers the Garda Stations located at Bridewell Dublin, Fitzgibbon Street, Mountjoy, Store Street, these Garda Stations reported the most crime in 2016.

Leinster Over The Years

If we take a look at the sum of crimes in Leinster over the years we see that the crime in Leinster peaked in 2008, and slowly went down from there, until it hit the lowest value of 31616 in 2016, a very sharp decrease in reported crime!
